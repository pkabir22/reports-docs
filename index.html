Daily Liquidity Report Daily
Treasury Team · Requested by ABL · ~30–40 min to produce (when all external data is available)
▼
This is ABL's most critical and complex daily report — a real-time command center for the Treasury team's liquidity management, covenant compliance, and capital markets decision-making. It was significantly re-engineered by Mahfuz to reduce manual effort, eliminate error-prone steps, and cut production time through Power Query automation and ongoing VBA macro development.
Purpose & Overview
A comprehensive daily snapshot of ABL's capital structure and liquidity position. Covers real-time warehouse (WH) utilization, liquidity runway, funding mix, and covenant compliance across all warehouses and securitization shelves. Used by the Treasury team for intraday decision-making, capital markets activity, and compliance monitoring.
Report Sections
Section 1 — Capital Structure & Liquidity Snapshot
Daily overview of total indebtedness, available capacity, leverage ratios, blended cost of capital, and ending cash position.
Section 2 — Securitizations (2 RTL Shelves)
Balances, cash held in payment/accumulation accounts, UPB surplus/shortfall, and associated funding costs per securitization shelf.
Section 3 — Warehouse Financing (Multi-Bank)
Facility limits, collateral pledged, outstanding balances, PVE, funding costs, and current cash-covenant status per lender/warehouse.
Section 4 — Daily Cash Flow Tracker & 29-Day Projection
Ties operating cash & PVE to funding activity, capital markets actions, payoffs, payroll, ending liquidity, and covenant coverage. Projects ending liquidity for the next 29 work days.

let
    source = CommonDataService.Database("abl1.crm.dynamics.com"),
    query = "
    SELECT
        s.new_loannumber1 as 'ln_id',
        o.new_los_property_address1 as 'address',
        s.new_spdate as 'ln_sold_date',
        o.new_loanamount as 'ln_amount',
        CASE
            WHEN o.new_escrowagentname = 'ABL RPC Dutch'
                THEN o.new_loanamount
                ELSE (
                    o.new_cashavailableforhousepurchaseatclose
                    + s.new_drawfundsdisbursed
                    + o.new_interestescrow
                    + o.new_escrowother
                    - s.new_blanketpropertypaydown
                )
        END AS 'upb',
        s.new_upb as 'crm_upb',
        o.new_cashavailableforhousepurchaseatclose as 'day1_upb',
        s.new_loanownername as 'owner',
        CASE
            WHEN o.new_loanpurposename LIKE '%Construction%'
                THEN 'Ground Up'
            WHEN o.new_loanpurposename LIKE '%Rehabilitation%'
                THEN 'Fix & Flip'
            ELSE 'Bridge'
        END AS 'ln_type',
        CASE
            WHEN o.new_loanpurposename LIKE '%Stabilized%'
                THEN 'ARM'
            ELSE 'Fixed'
        END AS 'ARM Flag',
        CASE
            WHEN o.new_loanpurposename LIKE '%Purchase%' THEN 'Purchase'
            WHEN o.new_loanpurposename LIKE '%Cash Out%' THEN 'CO Refi'
            WHEN o.new_loanpurposename LIKE '%Loan Refinance%' THEN 'RT Refi'
            WHEN o.new_loanpurposename = 'Refinance & New Construction' THEN 'RT Refi'
            WHEN o.new_loanpurposename IN ('Property New Construction Only',
                 'Property Rehabilitation Only') THEN 'RT Refi'
            ELSE ''
        END AS 'ln_purpose',
        REPLACE(o.new_escrowagentname,'ABL RPC','') as 'escrow_type'
    FROM opportunity as o
    LEFT JOIN new_postclose as s
        ON o.new_servicingfile = s.new_postcloseid
    WHERE s.new_loanownername = 'ABL2024RTL1'
        AND o.new_dealstagename <> 'Paid Off'
",
    myQuery = Value.NativeQuery(source, query)
in
    myQuery

let
    source = CommonDataService.Database("abl1.crm.dynamics.com"),
    query = "
    select
        distinct
            o.name as 'Property Address',
            cast(pr.new_scheduledclosedate as date) as 'Scheduled Close Date',
            pr.new_closingconfidencescorename as 'Projected Closing Confidence Score',
            cast(pr.new_processing_projected_close_date as date) as 'TOE Closing Date',
            o.new_dealstagename as 'Deal Stage',
            o.statecodename as 'Loan Status',
            p.statuscodename as 'Status',
            o.new_loanpurposename,
            case
                when o.new_loanpurposename like '%New Construction%' then 'GUC'
                else 'Non GUC'
            end as 'Loan Purpose',
            o.new_visableupb as 'UPB',
            case
                when o.new_loanpurposename like '%New Construction%'
                    then (-0.5 * o.new_visableupb)
                else (-0.15 * o.new_visableupb)
            end as 'Haircut'
    from opportunity as o
    left join new_processing as pr
        on o.opportunityid = pr.new_opportunity
    left join new_postclose as p
        on o.new_servicingfile = p.new_postcloseid
    where o.statecodename = 'Open'
        and pr.new_processing_projected_close_date >= cast(getdate() as date)
        and o.new_dealstagename in ('Clear To Close', 'Final Underwriting', 'Processing')
        and LOWER(o.name) NOT LIKE '%test%'
",
    response = Value.NativeQuery(source, query),
    #"Sorted Rows" = Table.Sort(response,{{"TOE Closing Date", Order.Ascending}}),
    #"Filtered Rows" = Table.SelectRows(#"Sorted Rows", each true)

Step-by-Step Production Process
  1. Refresh Dynamics CRM Power Queries — open the master workbook and run "Refresh All" to pull the latest data into the Bridge tab (RTL haircuts), Term tab (DSCR haircuts), RTL1 UPBs (2024-RTL1 and 2025-RTL1), and 1Sharpe UPB/Balance columns. Verify row counts are as expected.
  2. Load CFSB Daily Summary — confirm the Treasury team has uploaded the latest Daily RPC Sheet to Dropbox. Once confirmed, open via Get Data > From Excel Workbook, navigate to the ...\1_cfsb\Daily RPC Sheets\2026 folder, select the latest file and load the Daily Summary tab. Excel referencing then maps the relevant cells into the 1_Pager automatically.
  3. Load Nomura EOD Report — confirm the Treasury team has uploaded the Nomura EOD file to Dropbox. Once confirmed, open via Get Data > From Excel Workbook, navigate to ...\Nomura_EDO_M203s\[Month], select the latest file and load the Results tab. Excel referencing maps the values into the 1_Pager.
  4. Enter Mizuho EOD figures — open the Mizuho end-of-day email and manually enter the relevant figures directly into the appropriate cells in the 1_Pager.
  5. Calculate and enter Payoffs — open each Investor Notification Deposit PDF received via email. In each PDF, locate the Principal Payment column (6th column from the right) and take 22.5% of that figure. Once done for all PDFs, sum all the resulting values and enter the total into cell L23 in the 1_Pager.
  6. Enter Teams channel data — check the Teams channel for any DSCR Sale/Repo figures, RTL/Trust activity, and other capital/funding activity posted by Capital Markets or Treasury team members. Enter manually into the 1_Pager.
  7. Enter Operating Cash — log into the CFSB portal and calculate: SUM(Acct 5247 + Acct 5239 + Mor Dutch Esc + Acct 5352). Enter the result into the 1_Pager.
  8. Enter RTL1 PA & AA figures — log into the USB Pivot account portal and manually enter the Payment Account (PA) and Accumulation Account (AA) balances for both 2024-RTL1 and 2025-RTL1 into the 1_Pager.
  9. Verify formula propagation — confirm all Power Query refreshes and manual entries have correctly flowed through Excel formulas into the 1_Pager. Spot-check haircut projections for the current day and a few of the 29 forward work days.
  10. Validate covenant & liquidity figures — review all covenant cells for flags. Cross-check ending cash, total indebtedness, available capacity, WH utilization, and PVE against source data.

These are the core tools used across ABL's reporting suite. Each plays a distinct role — from automated data extraction to structured summaries and workflow automation.

Power Query
Used for automated data extraction and transformation from Dynamics CRM and Dropbox-hosted Excel files. Eliminates manual copy-paste and enables repeatable, refresh-on-click data loading. Central to Mahfuz's automation effort on the Daily Liquidity Report.
VBA Macros
Automates multi-step workflows — refreshing query sequences, formatting outputs, consolidating data across tabs, and enabling one-click execution for end users. Currently being developed to unify Power Query refreshes in the Daily Liquidity Report.
Excel Formulas & Referencing
Cross-sheet and cross-workbook references maintain data integrity between sections. Formulas drive key calculated metrics: leverage ratios, PVE, covenant thresholds, haircut projections, and 29-day liquidity forecasts.
Pivot Tables
Used in weekly and monthly reports to summarise large datasets by dimension (product type, region, time period). Enables fast slice-and-dice analysis without formula complexity.
Dynamics CRM
ABL's primary source for loan-level data: originations, balances, payoffs, and collateral attributes. Connected via Power Query for automated daily refresh across multiple report tabs.
Manual Entry
Required for data arriving from external parties — bank portals, Mizuho emails, PDF notices, and Teams channel posts. These sources are outside ABL's direct systems and cannot be automated without API or portal access.

Key terms used across ABL's treasury and finance reports. Intended for onboarding new team members and non-technical stakeholders reviewing the documentation.

TermDefinition
WH / WarehouseShort-term credit facility from a bank used to fund loan originations before they are sold or securitized.
PVEPurchase Value Excess — the amount by which the market/purchase value of collateral exceeds the outstanding warehouse balance. Represents a liquidity buffer.
UPBUnpaid Principal Balance — the remaining principal owed on a loan or pool of loans.
RTL ShelfResidential Transition Loan securitization shelf — a registered structure used to issue ABS backed by short-term bridge/fix-and-flip loans.
SecuritizationProcess of pooling loans and issuing securities backed by those loans to capital markets investors.
CovenantContractual requirement in a credit facility (e.g. minimum liquidity, max advance rate). Breach triggers lender remedies.
Blended Cost of CapitalWeighted average interest rate across all outstanding debt facilities (WH lines + securitizations).
Liquidity RunwayProjected number of days the company can operate and fund without needing new capital, based on current cash and PVE.
OriginationFunding of a new loan to a borrower, drawing on a warehouse facility.
DrawA borrowing request on an existing loan construction budget or committed facility.
DSCRDebt Service Coverage Ratio — a loan type for investment properties assessed on rental income rather than borrower income.
F&F / Fix & FlipShort-term bridge loan used to purchase and renovate a property before resale.
PA / AAPayment Account / Accumulation Account — trust accounts within a securitization structure that hold cash for bond payments or future deployment.
1_PagerThe master summary tab/output of the Daily Liquidity Report — the single consolidated view the Treasury team uses for daily decisions and presentations.
